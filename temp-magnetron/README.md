Beyond routine servicing, this project examines the engineering constraints,

## Technical Focus Areas

- Radar fundamentals and magnetron operation

- High-voltage DC power supply architecture

- RF transmission through waveguides and rotating joints

- Preventative maintenance strategies and diagnostic reasoning

- Relevance of marine radar systems to modern telecommunications engineering

## System Constraints

- High-voltage operation required for magnetron excitation places strict
requirements on insulation, grounding, and component spacing.

- Marine environmental exposure (salt spray, humidity, temperature cycling)
accelerates corrosion and dielectric degradation.

- Mechanical rotation of the antenna and waveguide introduces wear,
alignment sensitivity, and vibration-related faults.

- Frequency stability limitations inherent to magnetron-based systems
influence performance and maintenance intervals.

## Safety Considerations

- Presence of kilovolt-level DC power necessitates strict lockout,
discharge, and verification procedures.

- Stored energy in capacitors remains hazardous even after power-down.

- RF radiation exposure risks during operation require controlled
test conditions and awareness of safe distances.

- Marine-specific hazards such as moisture ingress and galvanic corrosion
increase electrical safety risks over time.
